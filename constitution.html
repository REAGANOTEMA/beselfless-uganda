CONSTITUTION

OF

BESELFLESS (U) LIMITED

The liability of the members is limited. This constitution establishes a robust framework for governance, objectives, powers, financial integrity, membership rights, and operational excellence of the company. It serves as the guiding document to ensure the organization achieves its mission of nurturing resilient, self-reliant, and empowered communities across Uganda.

1. Name and Office

The name of the Company shall be “BESELFLESS (U) LIMITED”. The registered office shall be located in Kasangalabi village, Ggulu ward, Mukono district, Uganda. The company may establish branches or offices elsewhere as deemed necessary by the Board of Directors.

2. Objectives

  • Provide education opportunities through scholarships, grants, mentorship, and access to learning resources both on campus and online.
  • Build resilient, sustainable, and self-reliant communities socially, economically, and spiritually.
  • Strengthen socio-economic resilience through the promotion of ethical work culture and entrepreneurship.
  • Mobilize resources via contributions, donations, grants, sponsorships, and income-generating ventures.
  • Encourage transparency, accountability, and ethical fundraising and lending practices.
  • Establish meaningful partnerships with individuals, organizations, governmental and non-governmental institutions.
  • Undertake initiatives to alleviate poverty, distress, and social inequality among disadvantaged communities.
  • Develop capacities of members for active societal participation and leadership roles.
  • Promote education, vocational training, and enterprise development through access to innovative resources.
  • Engage in profitable trade, property investment, and development ventures to sustain the company’s objectives.
  • Undertake any other lawful activities incidental or conducive to the achievement of the company’s objectives.

3. Powers of the Company

  • Borrow, receive, and secure loans in the best interest of the organization.
  • Mobilize financial, material, and human resources at local, national, and international levels.
  • Solicit grants and funding from governmental and non-governmental sources.
  • Employ, train, and maintain personnel, including pensions, insurance, and other benefits.
  • Establish endowment, building, and guarantee funds for long-term sustainability.
  • Publish educational and informational materials to advance the objectives of the company.
  • Invest in financial instruments and conduct strategic investments aligned with company objectives.
  • Form partnerships, operate trading entities, and acquire assets necessary for growth and development.
  • Advocate and lobby for policy, resources, and support in alignment with company goals.
  • Monitor, evaluate, and influence social, educational, and development programs impacting communities.

4. Membership

Membership is open to subscribers to the memorandum and other approved applicants. Membership shall be governed by transparency, accountability, and ethical practices.

  • Affiliate membership is open to firms, institutions, and individuals committed to the objectives of the company.
  • Termination of membership requires a minimum one-month written notice.
  • Upon termination, the company retains 5% of the retiring member's savings for administrative purposes and refunds the remainder within 60 days.
  • Members are expected to participate actively in organizational programs, governance meetings, and initiatives.

5. Board of Directors & Executive Roles

  • Formulate policies, governance frameworks, and strategic direction.
  • Approve capital expenditures and organizational budgets.
  • Assign and delegate duties to directors, managers, and staff.
  • Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations.

Executive Director

  • Supervise staff and program operations to achieve objectives efficiently.
  • Manage correspondence, communication with the board, and external stakeholders.
  • Assist in mobilization of funds and monitoring of all programs.

Program Manager

  • Oversee day-to-day operations and management of programs.
  • Ensure achievement of organizational objectives with efficiency and impact.
  • Support fundraising and reporting for development initiatives.

6. Finance & Accounts

  • Maintain bank accounts in reputable Ugandan financial institutions.
  • Finance sourced from donations, membership fees, fundraising, grants, and income-generating ventures.
  • All financial activities adhere to proper accounting, reporting, and audit practices for transparency and accountability.

7. Meetings

General Meetings

Annual and special general meetings will be held with proper notice, quorum, and voting procedures.

Board Meetings

Board meetings shall follow quorum requirements, voting rules, and delegation powers as specified in governance policies.

8. Amendments

Amendments to the constitution require a two-thirds majority vote of members in a duly convened general meeting, ensuring inclusivity and consensus.

9. Indemnity

Directors, officers, and auditors are indemnified against all liabilities incurred while performing duties in good faith.

10. Arbitration

All disputes between members or the company will be resolved through a single arbitrator or umpire under Ugandan arbitration laws, ensuring impartiality and fairness.
